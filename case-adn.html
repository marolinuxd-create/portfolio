ADN. Free, national, digital · 2020
01 · Problem

Popular outside its print footprint, invisible online.

By 2019, ADN was Colombia's first free newspaper, distributed in print across Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Barranquilla and Bucaramanga. Demand was growing in other regions but the print model was geographically capped.

The existing site was an afterthought: a place to download PDFs of the print edition. It didn't carry the brand, didn't function on mobile, and didn't help readers outside those 5 cities feel like they had access to the paper.

The brief, in one line

Redesign the website so anyone in Colombia gets ADN and the team has a digital product they can keep growing.

02 · My role

Lead designer, lead researcher, lead question asker.

I owned research and UX end-to-end. I worked with one UI designer, two developers, and a PM — plus stakeholders from editorial and commercial. My deliverables: Business Model Canvas, user personas, IA, MVP plan, wireframes, and clickable prototypes.

03 · Process

Know the product. Know the user. Then design.

01

Discovery — product + users in parallel

  • Built a Business Model Canvas with stakeholders to surface value creation, delivery, and capture levers.
  • Created user personas across reader archetypes (commuter, regional, casual, loyal).
  • Reviewed competitive digital editions in LATAM for IA patterns.
  • Ran interactive surveys on Instagram to capture sentiment from outside the print cities.
02

Definition — needs, then "How might we…"

Translated insights into internal (business) and external (reader) needs. Ran a "How Might We" ideation session with stakeholders, then drafted the IA and the MVP scope based on what could be shipped against real engineering constraints.

03

Prototyping — wireframes for home + sections

Wireframed the home page and the major sections. Built clickable prototypes for the home, section pages, and article view. Tested with readers from outside the 5 print cities — the audience the redesign was actually for.

04 · Key decisions

The four trade-offs that gave ADN a digital identity.

Decision 01

Mobile-first, even though print drove the brand.

Most readers outside the 5 print cities were on mobile. The wireframes were designed for the smallest screen first desktop was a stretch, not a starting point.

Decision 02

News, not PDFs.

The legacy site was a PDF library. We reframed it as a news product articles as articles, with proper IA, search, and section navigation.

Decision 03

One IA, room to grow.

The MVP shipped a small set of sections, but the IA was modeled to absorb new ones (regional, video, opinion) without rework.

Decision 04

Brand, finally.

The print brand was strong. The digital one didn't exist. We brought type, color, and editorial voice into the web product so it felt like reading ADN, not a generic news site.

05 · Before & after

From PDF library to a news product.

Before

diarioadn.com a list of PDFs.

Static page with downloadable editions. No mobile layout. No search. No way to find a specific story without downloading the whole paper.

After

diarioadn.com a national news product.

Mobile-first IA, section navigation, article pages with proper hierarchy, brand identity carried over from print. Readers in any region get the same access.

06 · Impact

What shipped and what kept shipping.

5 → ∞ cities reached

What I'd do differently

Spend less time on home-page hierarchy debates and more on article-page reading experience. The home is the front door, but readers spend their actual time on articles — that's where polish compounds.
